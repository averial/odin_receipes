Biryani Recipe

Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish, typically made with layers of aromatic basmati rice, marinated meat (chicken, goat, lamb, beef, or seafood), and a blend of spices. It's known for its complex flavor profile, often featuring ingredients like ginger, garlic, turmeric, chili, cumin, coriander, and caramelized onions. The dish is often cooked using the dum method, where it's sealed and slow-cooked to allow the flavors to meld together.

Ingredients

    Basmati rice 2 cups
    Chicken or Mutton 500 grams
    Yogurt ½ cup
    Onions – 2 large
    Tomatoes – 2 medium
    Cooking oil or ghee
    Fresh coriander
    Ginger-garlic paste – 2 tablespoons
    Fresh mint leaves – a handful
    Cloves – 4 to 6
    Green chilies – 2 to 4 (slit)
    Biryani masala
    Whole spices
    Red chili powder – 1 teaspoon

Steps

    Step 1

    Soak 2 cups of basmati rice for 30 minutes.Boil water with salt and whole spices (like bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves).Parboil the rice (about 70% cooked), drain, and set aside.

    Step 2

    In oil or ghee, fry sliced onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and cook until soft. Add yogurt, spices (red chili, turmeric, garam masala, biryani masala), salt, and cook for a few minutes. Add chicken or mutton and cook until tender (mutton takes longer).

    Step 3

    in a large pot, layer half the cooked rice. Add the meat masala over it, sprinkle chopped mint, coriander, and fried onions. Add the remaining rice on top. Drizzle saffron milk, lemon juice, and a few drops of kewra/rose water if available.
